Explore Košice

Historic streets, Gothic architecture, and the neighbourhoods that give the city its character.

Landmarks & Monuments

Monument

St. Elizabeth Cathedral

Slovakia's largest Gothic church, with an altar piece by the legendary Master MS. Climb the tower for panoramic city views.

Old Town • Cathedral
Culture

Košice State Theatre

A stunning Neo-Baroque theatre built in 1899. Catch an opera or ballet performance — the building alone is worth it.

Old Town • Performance
Architecture

Urban Nature — Kasárne

A former military barracks turned cultural hub. The best example in Central Europe of adaptive reuse of industrial heritage.

Downtown • Cultural
Historic

Main Street (Hlavná)

The city's grand promenade. Baroque facades, the iconic Singing Fountain, and a tempo that shifts from sleepy morning to buzzing evening.

Old Town • Promenade
Museum

East Slovak Gallery

Four floors of Slovak and Central European art in a beautifully restored burgher house. Free on the first Sunday of each month.

Old Town • Art
Nature

Košice City Park

Forty hectares of green in the city centre. The Hipokrates meadow, a lake, a children's railway, and the Summer stage for outdoor concerts.

Central • Park

Neighbourhoods

Old Town (Staré Mesto)

The heart of Košice — a pedestrian-friendly medieval core with a cathedral, theatre, endless bars and restaurants, and the energy of a city that actually lives here. Walk it in 20 minutes, spend a week getting to know it.

The Jewish Quarter (Kempelen's)

One of the best-preserved Jewish quarters in Central Europe. Quiet residential streets, a beautiful synagogue, and some of the city's best-kept secrets — if you know where to look.

Košice-Berg (Vyšné Opátske)

A leafy hillside suburb above the city centre. The 16th-century St. Anne Chapel has views that will make you question why you'd ever leave Košice at all.
